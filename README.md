  <p style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 22.5px;">
    Hacker Lab has been <span style="color: cornflowerblue;">operating for the past decade</span> in Sacramento, at
    times with multiple branches in neighboring communities. During that time they've <span
      style="color: cornflowerblue;">offered an enormous range of resources and services</span> that have helped to make
    Sacramento one of the most advantageous cities in California for aspiring artists, makers and creatively-inclined
    programmers.
  </p>

  <p style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 22.5px;">
    No other facility in Sacramento or any other city I've personally lived in compares to the range of Hacker Lab's
    resources, and <span style="color: cornflowerblue;">for many members there are truly no alternatives</span> in terms
    of fulfilling the practical creative needs these represent.
  </p>

  <p style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 22.5px;">
    When I first toured Hacker Lab I was legitimately shocked at the range of tools and equipment they had available. As
    someone who had moved to Sacramento relatively recently it was beyond inspiring and fundamentally changed my outlook
    for a future here.
  </p>

  <p style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 22.5px;">
    Again they have announced that <span style="color: cornflowerblue;">they will be selling off this equipment</span>
    in some form and if anyone had the capital necessary this could represent <span style="color: cornflowerblue;">a
      unique opportunity to acquire all the hardware necessary to stock a world-class makerspace</span> in one sweep
    rather than piecemeal. Of course it would make infinitely more sense to also take advantage of the space and devoted
    staff that also already exist and have been maintaining this equipment and training people on it for the past 10
    years.
  </p>
