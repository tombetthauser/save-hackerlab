Please Save Hacker Lab

This is a humble plea to any potential angel investors, philanthropists or socially-minded tech millionaires out there to please save Hacker Lab, a beloved world-class makerspace in Sacramento. Staff has announced the makerspace will begin closing at the end of July.

I suspect I am not alone in saying that Hacker Lab is without a doubt the most inspiring, professional and well-equipped makerspace that I have ever encountered. I'm a fairly new member and not an official representative of the space but as a remote tech worker and artist I'm finding it nearly impossible to imagine Sacramento's tech or arts culture without it and I desperately want it to survive in some form.

Hacker Lab has been operating for the past decade in Sacramento, at times with multiple branches in neighboring communities. During that time they've offered an enormous range of services to help individuals and small independent businesses.

  • memberships for 24 hour facility access
  • open desks and workbenches in large shared work spaces
  • clean and well-stocked shared kitchen area
  • enormous wood and metal shops
  • ridiculously wide range of tools and equipment
  • separate photography room and 3D printer room
  • public training classes for using equipment
  • private shop lockers for personal equipment
  • private desk and office rentals for small businesses
  • devoted front desk and administrative staff

When I first toured Hacker Lab I was legitimately shocked at the range of tools and equipment they had available. Again they have announced that they will be auctioning off all this equipment and if anyone had the capital necessary this could represent an unique opportunity to acquire all the hardware necessary to stock a world-class makerspace in one sweep rather than piecemeal.
